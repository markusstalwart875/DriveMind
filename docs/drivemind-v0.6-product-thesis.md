# DriveMind v0.6 — Product Thesis

日期：2026-03-22  
状态：重构中轴 / 新作品定义

---

## One-line judgment

`DriveMind` should not remain a “calm reliability layer”.
That framing is mature, but too soft to carry a real `0.6` work.

If `DriveMind` is to become a true product, it should be rebuilt as:

# an execution-integrity layer for long-horizon human-AI work

And more fully:

# the layer that keeps human-AI collaboration from degrading during meaningful work, while turning real work into reusable future strength

---

## Why the current v0.5 still feels like 0.4x

The current work already has many good ingredients:
- persistence
- decision gates
- escalation rules
- task typing
- session handoff
- review and distillation
- reusable lessons

But those ingredients are still arranged more like:
- a mature collaboration doctrine
- a calm execution philosophy
- a good-agent constitution

That makes the work sound thoughtful, but not yet non-substitutable.

It explains how a good agent should behave.
It does not yet sharply answer:

# what concrete high-cost failure mode this product exists to defeat

That is why the work feels closer to `0.4–0.45` than to a true `0.5` or `0.6`.

---

## The real enemy

The enemy is not “lack of effort”.
The enemy is not “lack of calm”.
The enemy is not even “lack of review”.

The enemy is:

# degradation during long-horizon work

That degradation shows up in at least five recurring forms:

### 1. Goal drift
The task starts well, but slowly drifts away from its real objective.
The agent keeps moving, but no longer on the right thing.

### 2. Boundary drift
Urgency, frustration, importance, or partial permission are gradually mistaken for full authorization.

### 3. Continuity decay
Across session breaks, long chains, or context pressure, the execution state loses fidelity.
The work resumes, but the real reasoning thread is damaged.

### 4. Stuck degeneration
When blocked, the agent shifts from diagnosis into fake motion:
- mechanical retries
- random path switching
- activity without clarified blocker

### 5. Closure failure
The task ends without producing durable residue:
- no lesson
- no next-time rule
- no trustworthy handoff state
- no compounding effect for future work

A real `DriveMind 0.6` should be judged by whether it pushes these failure modes down.

---

## New product center

The new center should be:

# long-horizon execution integrity

But that is still incomplete unless it also includes compounding.

So the fuller center is:

# execution integrity + experience compounding

Meaning:
- preserve objective integrity
- preserve boundary integrity
- preserve continuity fidelity
- preserve diagnostic quality under friction
- preserve closure quality
- turn completed work into stronger future defaults

This is what makes DriveMind more than a collaboration style.
It becomes a systems layer for keeping work from degrading and for making real work cumulative.

---

## The new product claim

DriveMind v0.6 should claim:

# It helps human-AI collaboration stay aligned, bounded, and recoverable during meaningful work — and leave behind reusable strength afterward.

This is stronger than:
- calm
- steady
- reliable

Those are temperamental qualities.
This is an operational claim.

---

## The four-stage collaboration loop

The old framing scattered too much across behavior categories.
The new framing should organize around a genuine collaboration loop:

### 1. Take in the task
Convert intention into a stable working objective.
The goal here is not just “understand the task,” but prevent bad starts and hidden ambiguity from poisoning the rest of the work.

### 2. Advance the flow
Keep the task moving without losing objective, boundary, or judgment quality.
This is where anti-drift and anti-fake-motion behavior live.

### 3. Participate in execution
Do not merely observe or narrate. Contribute at the points where judgment and action actually change the result.

### 4. Distill the experience
Turn real work into reusable future advantage:
- lesson
- next-time rule
- handoff state
- memory
- SOP residue when appropriate

This is the compounding engine.
Without it, the work may be completed, but the system does not actually evolve.

---

## Reframing the existing modules

Current components are not wrong. They are just not grouped under a strong enough product center.

Under `v0.6`, they should be reorganized like this:

### A. Drift Prevention
- task typing
- confidence signaling
- progress continuity
- objective anchoring

### B. Boundary Preservation
- decision gates
- escalation rules
- explicit human authority
- pause-before-risk logic

### C. Continuity Preservation
- session handoff
- persistent state discipline
- resume fidelity
- diary / distill linkage

### D. Stuck Recovery
- blocker diagnosis
- bounded alternative search
- smallest useful next action
- anti-fake-motion behavior

### E. Closure & Compounding
- review
- reusable lesson
- next-time rule
- durable memory residue

This reorganization would make DriveMind feel much more like a product engine and much less like a constitutional essay.

---

## What must change in the skill itself

If this thesis is real, the skill cannot stay as a long-form collaboration doctrine.

`skill/SKILL.md` should become much more skill-native:
- shorter
- sharper
- more trigger-specific
- more runtime-oriented
- less manifesto-like

The skill body should focus on:
1. trigger signals for degradation risk
2. the shortest active path to restore execution integrity
3. when to load deeper references
4. how to leave behind compounding residue after meaningful work

The body should not attempt to fully explain the philosophy.
That belongs in references and docs.

---

## What success would look like

DriveMind v0.6 would feel real only if users could notice outcomes like:

- long tasks drift less often
- blocked work produces clearer blocker diagnosis instead of frantic motion
- pressure does not silently erode authorization boundaries
- resuming after interruption preserves more of the actual thread
- completed work leaves behind better future defaults, not just a summary

Without these effects, the product remains intellectually attractive but operationally soft.

---

## What v0.6 is not

DriveMind v0.6 is not:
- a generic maturity upgrade for all agents
- a broad collaboration etiquette layer
- a manifesto about working well
- a memory system by itself
- a planning tool by itself

It is:

# the layer that resists degradation and compounds experience across meaningful human-AI work

---

## Bottom line

A true `DriveMind 0.6` should no longer be judged by whether it sounds calm, careful, and mature.

It should be judged by whether it meaningfully reduces long-horizon collaboration failure and turns completed work into stronger future collaboration.

That is the standard that would make it feel like a real work, not a refined doctrine.
