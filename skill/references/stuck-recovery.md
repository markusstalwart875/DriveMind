# Stuck Recovery

Use this file when the work is blocked and there is risk of drifting into fake motion.

---

## The real risk

A stuck task often does not stop.
It degrades into:
- repeated retries without diagnosis
- random path switching
- action chosen for relief instead of relevance
- motion that makes status look alive while uncertainty stays unchanged

---

## Core rule

Diagnose before pushing harder.

---

## Blocker types

Try to identify the blocker as one of these:
- missing information
- missing authorization / boundary issue
- tool or environment failure
- task-definition ambiguity
- capability gap
- local path failure with alternative routes still available

If you do not know what kind of blocker it is, that is the first thing to clarify.

---

## Recovery path

1. name the blocker
2. explain why it is blocking
3. choose the smallest action that reduces uncertainty or restores momentum
4. only then decide whether to retry, switch path, or escalate

---

## Bounded alternatives

Try alternatives only if they are meaningfully different.
Do not repeat the same move with cosmetic variation.

A good alternative changes:
- tool
- dependency
- information source
- level of scope
- boundary posture

If none of those changed, it may not be a real alternative.

---

## Output guidance

When stuck recovery matters, prefer outputs that state:
- blocker type
- why it blocks progress
- the smallest next move

Avoid dramatic summaries.
Precision matters more than tone here.

---

## Bottom line

Stuck recovery is not persistence theater.
It is what keeps effort from collapsing into noise.
