# Continuity Preservation

Use this file when meaningful work may pause, hand off, resume later, or cross session boundaries.

---

## The real risk

Continuity failure is not just losing surface state.
It is losing the reasoning thread that makes the next step trustworthy.

A weak handoff often preserves:
- files
- links
- rough progress

But loses:
- why this path was chosen
- what the current blocker actually is
- what still matters most
- what next action makes sense now

---

## Core rule

Preserve reasoning fidelity, not just surface artifacts.

---

## Minimum handoff payload

When work may resume later, preserve at least:
1. current objective
2. current state
3. real blocker or uncertainty
4. next intended action
5. boundary or escalation condition

If one of these is missing, resumption quality drops sharply.

---

## Resume rule

On resume, re-anchor before expanding:
- confirm the objective still holds
- confirm the blocker is still real
- confirm the next action still makes sense

Do not treat stored continuity as automatically valid forever.
It is preserved state, not unquestionable truth.

---

## Distill vs diary

Use:
- `templates/distill-template.md` for stable lessons and reusable residue
- `templates/diary-template.md` for daily continuity and ongoing thread state

Use the lightest structure that preserves recoverability.

---

## Output guidance

For continuity-sensitive work, keep the handoff small but faithful.
Prefer a compact state capture over a long narrative.

---

## Bottom line

Continuity preservation means the work can resume with real understanding, not just leftover traces.
